# Quitting Tomorrow

![Platform](https://img.shields.io/badge/platform-iOS-lightgrey.svg)

## Overview

**Quitting Tomorrow** is an iOS app designed to help users release work-related stress daily by expressing their emotions. It provides a simple yet effective way to track and manage workplace frustrations through an interactive button interface.

## Description

Simply put, the app allows users to release work-related stress daily by expressing their emotions. It features a button with a counter function. The logic is that if a user clicks the button too many times in a day, it indicates a strong desire to quit their job.

## Features

- **Daily Stress Relief**: Express your work-related emotions through a simple tap
- **Smart Counter System**: Tracks how many times you click the button each day
- **Emotion Indicator**: The app monitors your clicks to gauge your desire to quit
- **Daily Reset**: Counter resets each day for a fresh start
- **User-Friendly Interface**: Simple and intuitive design focused on stress release

## How It Works

1. Open the app when you're feeling work-related stress
2. Tap the button to express your frustration
3. The app counts your daily clicks
4. If you click too many times in a day, the app recognizes a strong desire to quit your job
5. Use the insights to better understand your work satisfaction levels

## Purpose

This app serves as:
- A stress release mechanism for daily workplace frustrations
- A personal mood tracker to monitor work satisfaction over time
- A gentle reminder that "tomorrow is another day"

## Requirements

- iOS device (iPhone/iPad)
- iOS version compatible with the app

## Installation

1. Clone the repository
2. Open the project in Xcode
3. Build and run on your iOS device or simulator

## Usage

Simply launch the app and start tapping the button whenever you feel work-related stress. The app will track your daily usage and help you understand your emotional patterns regarding your job.

## Privacy

All data is stored locally on your device. Your stress levels and button clicks remain private and are never shared.
